MMS-CMRIS 2019

Multi-sequence Cardiac MRI Segmentation Challenge

Clinical Background
Accurate computing, analysis and modeling of the ventricles from 3D medical images is important in the development of clinical applications. Segmentation of ventricles and myocardium is however still arduous, particularly due to the pathological myocardium from LGE CMR; but manual delineation is generally time-consuming, tedious and subject to inter- and intra-observer variations.
Motivation
This challenge provides 45 multi-sequence CMR images from patients who underwent cardiomyopathy; and each patient had been scanned using the three CMR sequences, i.e. the LGE, T2 and bSSFP. It aims at creating an open and fair competition for various research groups to test and validate their methods, particularly for the multi-sequence ventricle and myocardium segmentation. The workshop is not only to benchmark various heart segmentation algorithms, but also to cover the topic of general cardiac image segmentation, registration and modeling.
Topic
The segmentation of ventricles and myocardium, including
Atlas based segmentation
Statistical shape model based segmentation
Deep learning segmentation Other methods
General cardiac image segmentation
Registration and modeling for cardiac images
Award
The best works will be awarded with prizes funded by Nvidia and the Arterys.com; and the selected papers will be published in Lecture Notes in Computer Science (LNCS), Springer with STACOM workshop (see previous proceedings). In addition, the benchmarked algorithms will be summarized to form a full paper for potential publications in MedIA and IEEE TMI, and the participants will share the credit.
